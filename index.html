Frequently Asked Questions

After a commercial vehicle accident, you should:

  • Seek immediate medical attention, even if injuries seem minor
  • Contact law enforcement to file an official report
  • Document the scene with photos and videos if possible
  • Exchange information with the other driver(s), including insurance details
  • Collect contact information from any witnesses
  • Notify your insurance company about the accident
  • Contact an experienced commercial vehicle accident attorney as soon as possible

At Claim Connectors, we work on a no-cost referral basis. You pay nothing to be connected with an attorney. Our partner attorneys work on a contingency fee basis, which means they only get paid if they secure compensation for you. Their fee is typically a percentage of the settlement or verdict amount. This arrangement ensures that everyone can afford quality legal representation, regardless of their financial situation.

The statute of limitations for filing a claim after a commercial vehicle accident varies by state, typically ranging from 1-3 years from the date of the accident. However, it's crucial to contact an attorney as soon as possible after an accident. Early intervention allows us to preserve evidence, interview witnesses while memories are fresh, and ensure all filing deadlines are met. Additionally, claims against government entities often have much shorter notice requirements, sometimes as little as 30-90 days.

Victims of commercial vehicle accidents may be eligible for various types of compensation, including:

  • Medical expenses (past and future)
  • Lost wages and lost earning capacity
  • Pain and suffering
  • Emotional distress
  • Property damage
  • Rehabilitation costs
  • In some cases, punitive damages

The specific compensation available will depend on the details of your case, the severity of your injuries, and other factors. Our attorneys will work to maximize your recovery across all applicable categories.

Commercial vehicle accident cases are significantly more complex than typical auto accidents for several reasons:

  • They involve federal regulations (FMCSA) that don't apply to regular drivers
  • Multiple parties may be liable (driver, trucking company, vehicle manufacturer, etc.)
  • Commercial vehicles are typically covered by large insurance policies with aggressive defense teams
  • Evidence specific to commercial vehicles (logbooks, electronic logging devices, maintenance records) requires specialized knowledge to properly analyze

Claim Connectors partners with attorneys who specialize in commercial vehicle accident cases and understand how to navigate these complexities to build the strongest possible case for you.
